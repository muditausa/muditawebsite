What is Mudita?

The Artist Collective of Tomorrow, Bringing the Possibility of Art & Music to the Central Florida Community

Video Description

Overview

In 2019, the nonprofit Mudita was established in Japan to share diverse art from around the world in the most accessible way with Japanese youth through the idea of “empathy.” After successful years in Japan with innovative missions and programs, the American branch -- Mudita America Inc. -- aims to expand Mudita’s shared vision of spreading empathetic connections through art experiences.

November 25, 2023

Union: A Cultural Narrative presented at the Orlando Rental Studio 

Join us for an unique evening of cross-cultural collaboration featuring music and dance of multiple cultures! Mudita America brings four prominent artists of today to Orlando who will each bring their unique artistic practice for this merger. Marin Naruse: a Japanese traditional singer and Sanshin player (uta-sha) from Amami Oshima Island, Japan; Eric Rokni: a cellist of Jewish and Persian upbringing; Tanuja Krishna: Indian Classical dancer; and Marcinko Orozco, a hip-hip influenced keyboard player. Their work on this project will showcase an extraordinary story of oppression and unification.

Our Mission

To cocreate intercultural opportunity, where all voices of the community are heard equally


Core Values

Collaboration
No man is an island; We believe that the start of any successful artistic experience begins with engaging with others to create something greater than the sum of its parts
Innovation
We explore new possibilities in art, and seek to uncover unique approaches to address current issues in our ever-diversifying society
Accessibility
We invite members of the community to participate in projects that current educational models overlook
Empathy
We facilitate global relationships through the arts and music, to help gain a better understanding of each other and create a tightly knit family
